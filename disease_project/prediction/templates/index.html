Understanding the Input Parameters

1. Pregnancies: The number of times a woman has been pregnant. It is an important factor in diabetes risk assessment.

2. Glucose Level: The amount of glucose present in the blood. High glucose levels may indicate diabetes.

3. Blood Pressure: The pressure exerted by circulating blood on the walls of blood vessels. Normal blood pressure is around 120/80 mm Hg.

4. Skin Thickness: The thickness of the skin fold in millimeters. It is used to estimate body fat percentage.

5. Insulin Level: The concentration of insulin in the blood. Insulin helps regulate blood sugar levels, and abnormal levels may indicate diabetes.

6. BMI (Body Mass Index): A measure of body fat based on weight and height. It is calculated as weight (kg) / height² (m²).

7. Diabetes Pedigree Function: A score indicating the likelihood of diabetes based on family history. A higher score means a greater genetic risk.

8. Age: The age of the individual. Older individuals have a higher risk of developing diabetes and other health conditions.
